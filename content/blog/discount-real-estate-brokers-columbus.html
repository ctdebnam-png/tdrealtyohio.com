Understanding the Different Discount Real Estate Models

Before comparing specific companies, let's understand the five main discount real estate models you'll encounter in Columbus:

1. Flat-Fee MLS Services

How it works: You pay a one-time fee (typically $300-$500) to get your home listed on the MLS. After that, you're essentially selling FSBO with MLS exposure.

What's included:

  • MLS listing entry
  • Syndication to Zillow, Realtor.com, etc.
  • Sometimes a yard sign

What's NOT included:

  • Pricing guidance or CMA
  • Professional photography
  • Showing coordination
  • Offer negotiation
  • Contract preparation/review
  • Closing coordination

Best for: Experienced sellers comfortable handling their own transaction who just need MLS exposure.

Potential pitfalls: Pricing mistakes, negotiation losses, and legal issues can easily exceed any commission savings.

2. 1% Commission Brokerages (Full Service)

How it works: You pay 1% of your sale price for complete listing agent services. This is TD Realty Ohio's model.

What's included:

  • Everything a traditional agent provides
  • Professional photography and marketing
  • Showing coordination
  • Expert negotiations
  • Closing support
  • At TD Realty: FREE pre-listing inspection

Best for: Sellers who want professional representation and significant savings without DIY hassles.

Considerations: Ensure the brokerage truly offers full service—some "1% brokers" actually provide limited services.

3. 1.5% Commission Brokerages

How it works: A middle-ground option charging 1.5% listing commission. Some national brands and local brokerages use this model.

What's included: Varies by company—could be full service or limited service.

Best for: Sellers in markets where 1% options don't exist or for higher-end homes where the company has a minimum fee.

4. 2% Commission Brokerages

How it works: You pay 2% listing commission instead of the traditional 2.5-3%.

What's included: Usually full service, as the rate is only slightly below traditional.

Best for: Sellers who can't qualify for lower-tier rates (e.g., TD Realty's 2% rate for sell-only clients who aren't buying through us).

5. Hybrid/Tiered Models

How it works: Companies offer different service levels at different price points. You might pay $999 for basic service or $2,999 for premium, regardless of home price.

Best for: Higher-priced homes where flat fees become much cheaper than percentage-based commissions.

What to Look for in a Columbus Discount Broker

Not all discount brokers are created equal. Here's how to evaluate your options:

1. True Cost Breakdown

Ask for a complete list of all fees. Some "discount" brokers advertise low commissions but add fees for:

  • Photography ($150-$400)
  • Lockbox ($50-$100)
  • Yard sign ($50-$100)
  • Transaction coordination ($300-$500)
  • Administrative fees ($200-$400)
  • MLS listing fee ($50-$200)

A 1% commission with $1,000 in add-on fees is really 1.25%. Get the full picture before signing.

2. Service Inclusions

Specifically ask:

  • Is professional photography included or DIY?
  • Who handles showing requests—you or them?
  • Will they personally present and negotiate offers?
  • What happens if the deal hits problems during inspection or appraisal?
  • Is closing coordination included?

3. Local Expertise

Columbus neighborhoods vary dramatically. A Westerville expert may not know Upper Arlington pricing nuances. Ask:

  • How many homes have you sold in my specific area?
  • What's your average sale-to-list price ratio?
  • Can you provide references from my neighborhood?

4. Communication and Availability

Selling a home involves time-sensitive decisions. Ensure your discount broker:

  • Responds promptly to inquiries
  • Is available for showings and open houses
  • Can be reached when offers come in (evenings/weekends)
  • Communicates proactively, not just reactively

5. Reviews and Track Record

Check Google reviews, Zillow reviews, and ask for references. Look for:

  • Recent reviews (last 6-12 months)
  • Reviews mentioning your specific area
  • How the broker responds to negative reviews
  • Patterns in feedback (good and bad)

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of discount real estate brokers are available in Columbus?

Columbus offers several discount models: flat-fee MLS ($300-$500 for listing only), 1% commission brokers (full service at 1% of sale price), 1.5-2% brokers, and hybrid models. Each offers different levels of service and savings.

Which discount realtor model offers the best value?

For most sellers, a full-service 1% commission broker offers the best value. You receive professional representation—photography, marketing, negotiations, closing support—at significant savings compared to traditional 3% agents, without the DIY burden of flat-fee services.

Are there hidden fees with discount real estate brokers?

Some discount brokers charge additional fees for photography, lockboxes, signage, or transaction coordination. Always ask for a complete fee breakdown before signing. Reputable discount brokers like TD Realty Ohio include all standard services in their quoted commission rate.

How do I choose between different discount brokers in Columbus?

Compare: 1) Total cost including all fees, 2) Services included, 3) Local expertise and reviews, 4) Communication style, and 5) What happens if issues arise. The cheapest option isn't always the best value if it leads to a lower sale price or more stress.
