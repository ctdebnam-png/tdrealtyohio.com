Traditional Real Estate Commissions vs. Discount Models

Before we dive into your options, let's understand what you're trying to save on. In a traditional Columbus home sale:

  • Listing agent commission: 2.5-3% of sale price
  • Buyer's agent commission: 2.5-3% of sale price (often paid by seller)
  • Total: 5-6% of your home's value

On a $400,000 Columbus home, that's $20,000-$24,000 in commissions alone. For many sellers, this exceeds their down payment on their next home.

The FSBO Reality: Is Selling Without an Agent Worth It?

For Sale By Owner sounds appealing—pay no commission and keep all your equity. But before you plant that sign, consider the data:

According to the National Association of Realtors:

  • FSBO homes sold for a median of $310,000 vs. $405,000 for agent-assisted sales in 2023
  • Only 7% of home sales were FSBO
  • FSBO sellers spent an average of 3 weeks longer on market

Now, some of this gap is due to FSBO sellers often knowing their buyers personally (family sales, etc.). But even accounting for that, studies consistently show FSBO homes sell for 5-7% less than comparable agent-listed properties.

Why FSBO Often Backfires

Pricing mistakes: Without access to comparable sales data and market expertise, FSBO sellers often price too high (causing the home to sit) or too low (leaving money on the table).

Limited exposure: You can't list on the MLS without an agent or flat-fee service. This means missing 90%+ of buyers who search through their agents.

Negotiation disadvantages: Buyers and their agents know FSBO sellers lack representation. This often leads to lowball offers and aggressive inspection negotiations.

Legal complexity: Ohio real estate contracts are legally binding documents. One mistake can cost you thousands or even kill the deal.

Time investment: Responding to inquiries, scheduling showings, hosting open houses, qualifying buyers—it's essentially a part-time job.

The math often doesn't work out. If FSBO costs you 5% in sale price, you've lost more than you "saved" on commission. That's why most sellers find that a discount realtor offers better net results.

Flat-Fee MLS: The Middle Ground (With Caveats)

Flat-fee MLS services let you list your home on the Columbus MLS for a one-time fee (typically $300-$500). Your listing then appears on Zillow, Realtor.com, and other sites, giving you exposure to buyers.

What you get:

  • MLS listing with your photos and description
  • Syndication to real estate websites
  • Sometimes a yard sign

What you don't get:

  • Pricing guidance
  • Professional photography
  • Showing coordination
  • Offer negotiation
  • Contract review
  • Inspection negotiations
  • Closing coordination

Flat-fee MLS is essentially FSBO with better marketing. You're still handling everything yourself—you just have MLS exposure while doing it.

This can work for experienced sellers who've been through multiple transactions. But for most Columbus homeowners, the learning curve and time investment make this option more costly than it appears.

Hidden Costs of Flat-Fee MLS

Consider these potential expenses:

  • Professional photography: $150-$400
  • Attorney review (recommended): $300-$500
  • Pricing mistakes: Potentially thousands in lost value
  • Negotiation losses: Often 1-3% of sale price
  • Your time: Dozens of hours over weeks or months

When you add these up, flat-fee MLS often costs as much as a 1% commission realtor—but without the professional support.

Discount Realtor Models: Understanding Your Options

The term "discount realtor" covers several different business models. Here's what you'll find in the Columbus market:

1. Limited-Service Discount Agents

Some discount agents offer a lower commission but provide fewer services. You might get MLS listing and basic paperwork, but limited marketing, showing support, or negotiation help. Always ask exactly what's included.

2. Volume-Based Discount Brokerages

These companies handle many transactions with a lean staff, passing savings to clients. Service quality varies—some are excellent, others feel like assembly lines. Research reviews carefully.
