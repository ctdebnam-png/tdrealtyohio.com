Agent commissions represent the largest selling cost—and the area where you have the most control. Understanding your options can save you thousands.

Traditional Commission Structure

Buyer's Agent Commission: What You Need to Know

Recent industry changes (NAR settlement) have made buyer's agent commission more negotiable. As a seller, you can:

  • Offer any amount to buyer's agents (including $0)
  • Let buyers negotiate their own agent's compensation
  • Continue offering traditional rates to attract more buyers

Our recommendation: In the current Columbus market, offering a competitive buyer's agent commission (2-2.5%) typically results in more showings and stronger offers. Seller Closing Costs in Columbus, Ohio

Beyond commissions, sellers face various closing costs that typically total 1-3% of the sale price. Here's what to expect:

Title Insurance

Cost: $1,000 - $2,500 (varies with sale price)

In Ohio, sellers typically pay for the owner's title insurance policy, which protects the buyer against title defects. This is a one-time fee based on the sale price.

Real Estate Transfer Tax

Cost: $4.00 per $1,000 of sale price (Franklin County)

Ohio charges a state conveyance fee, plus counties can add their own tax. In Franklin County (Columbus), the total is typically $4.00 per $1,000.

  • $300,000 home: $1,200
  • $400,000 home: $1,600
  • $500,000 home: $2,000

Prorated Property Taxes

Cost: Varies

You'll pay your share of property taxes up to the closing date. Columbus-area property taxes average 1.5-2% of home value annually, so budget accordingly.

Attorney Fees

Cost: $300 - $600

While not required in Ohio, many sellers hire a real estate attorney to review documents. This is especially recommended for complex transactions.

Recording Fees

Cost: $50 - $150

Administrative fees to record the deed transfer with the county.

Payoff Costs

Cost: Varies

If you have a mortgage, there may be fees to process the payoff, including wire transfer fees ($25-50) and any prepayment penalties (check your loan terms).

HOA Fees

Cost: $100 - $500

If your property is in an HOA, expect fees for estoppel letters, transfer fees, and prorated dues.

Hidden and Often-Overlooked Costs

Beyond the obvious expenses, watch for these costs that catch sellers off guard:

Mortgage Payoff Balance

Your remaining mortgage balance comes out of sale proceeds. Make sure you know your current payoff amount (which includes accrued interest).

Concessions to Buyers

Cost: 0-3% of sale price

Buyers often request seller concessions for closing costs or repairs found during inspection. Budget 1-2% as a contingency.

Overlap/Carrying Costs

If you buy before selling (or carry the home longer than expected), you'll face double mortgage payments, utilities, insurance, etc. Each month of overlap on a $400,000 home might cost $3,000+.

Capital Gains Tax

If your profit exceeds $250,000 (single) or $500,000 (married filing jointly) and you've lived in the home 2+ of the last 5 years, you may owe capital gains tax. Most Columbus sellers don't hit these thresholds, but verify with a tax professional.

Moving Costs

Cost: $1,000 - $5,000+

Local Columbus moves typically run $500-$2,000 for professional movers. Long-distance or large homes cost more.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to sell a house in Columbus, Ohio?

The total cost to sell a house in Columbus typically ranges from 8-10% of the sale price. This includes agent commissions (3-6%), closing costs (1-3%), and potential repair/prep costs (1-2%). On a $400,000 home, expect $32,000-$40,000 in total selling costs with a traditional agent.

What are the closing costs for sellers in Ohio?

Ohio sellers typically pay 1-3% of the sale price in closing costs, including title insurance ($1,000-$2,000), transfer taxes (0.4% in Franklin County), attorney fees ($300-$500), prorated property taxes, and various administrative fees.
