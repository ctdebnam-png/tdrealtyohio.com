Schools in Westerville

Westerville City Schools serves most of the city with consistently high ratings. The district operates multiple elementary schools, middle schools, and three comprehensive high schools: Westerville North, Central, and South.

Boundary Note: While most of Westerville is served by Westerville City Schools, some areas near I-270 fall within Columbus City Schools or Gahanna-Jefferson boundaries. Homes in Blendon Township may also be zoned differently. Always verify school assignments before purchasing.

Notable programs: The district offers STEM and gifted programs at multiple schools. Westerville North, Central, and South high schools each have distinct personalities and strong athletics programs. The Emerson Vocational Academy provides career-technical education options.

Neighborhoods in Westerville

Westerville offers diverse neighborhoods ranging from historic homes near Uptown to newer construction in surrounding townships.

Uptown Westerville

The heart of Westerville with tree-lined streets, historic charm, and walkable access to shops, restaurants, and Otterbein University. Character homes from early 1900s mixed with newer infill.

$350K - $550K No HOA Walkable

Genoa Woods

Established 1980s-90s subdivision with mature trees and larger lots. Popular with families for its quiet streets and proximity to Genoa Middle School. Mix of colonials and ranches.

$400K - $550K HOA 1/3+ acre lots

Heritage Lakes

Newer community (2000s) with contemporary floor plans, community pool, and walking trails around scenic lakes. Active HOA maintains common areas and organizes events.

$450K - $650K Active HOA Pool & trails

Huber Ridge

Affordable 1960s ranch-style homes on the south side. Great starter home opportunity with larger lots than typical. Some homes have been extensively updated.

$250K - $350K No HOA Columbus Schools

Olde Westerville

Victorian and craftsman homes near the historic downtown. Some of the most distinctive architecture in the city. Walking distance to the farmers market and community events.

$400K - $700K No HOA Historic district
