Schools in Delaware

Delaware City Schools serves the city of Delaware and surrounding areas with Hayes High School as the district's comprehensive high school. The district balances small-town values with quality education.

Growing District: As Delaware County grows, the school district is expanding while maintaining its community-focused culture. Ohio Wesleyan University in downtown provides unique educational partnership opportunities.

Notable features: Strong community support, newer facilities in growing areas, and partnership opportunities with Ohio Wesleyan. The district offers good value compared to higher-priced suburban districts.

Neighborhoods in Delaware

Delaware offers everything from historic downtown charm to new construction communities, with a mix of city and rural township options.

Historic Downtown Delaware

Charming downtown with brick streets, local shops, and Ohio Wesleyan University. Victorian and craftsman homes near Sandusky Street. Walking distance to restaurants and farmers market.

$300K - $500K Walkable Historic character

The Landings

Newer planned community with variety of home styles, community amenities, and walking trails. Popular with families seeking new construction at reasonable prices.

$350K - $500K HOA New construction

Briarwood

Established neighborhood with mature trees and well-maintained homes. Mix of styles from 1980s-2000s. Good schools and community feel. Popular starter home area.

$275K - $400K Established Family-friendly

South Delaware (near Polaris)

Rapidly developing area with new construction communities, easy access to Polaris shopping and I-71. Best of both worlds - Delaware address with Columbus convenience.

$400K - $600K New construction Near Polaris

Rural Delaware Township

Acreage properties and rural living while still within Delaware schools. Mix of older farmhouses and newer custom builds. Great for those wanting space and privacy.

$400K - $800K Acreage available Rural character
