The Selling Process

Click each step to learn exactly what happens and what you'll need to do.

What We'll Cover

  • Your timeline: When you need to sell, whether you're buying another home, and any coordination needed
  • Commission rate: Confirm whether you qualify for 1% (buying + selling) or 2% (sell only)
  • Market analysis: Review comparable sales and discuss realistic pricing expectations
  • Property condition: Walk through your home to identify any preparation needed

What You'll Need to Decide

  • Target listing date
  • Whether to make repairs/updates before listing
  • Showing availability and restrictions
Pro Tip

Start the conversation even if you're months away from listing. Early planning gives you time to make strategic improvements that maximize your sale price.

What Gets Inspected

  • Structure: Foundation, framing, roof condition
  • Systems: HVAC, electrical, plumbing
  • Exterior: Siding, windows, drainage, grading
  • Interior: Walls, ceilings, floors, doors, stairs
  • Safety: Smoke detectors, carbon monoxide, handrails

After the Report

You'll have three options for any issues found:

  • Fix it: Make repairs before listing to command top price
  • Price it: Adjust asking price to reflect the condition
  • Disclose it: Sell as-is with full disclosure to buyers
Why This Matters

80% of deals that fall apart do so because of inspection surprises. Knowing issues upfront lets you price accurately and negotiate from strength.

Marketing Package Includes

  • Professional photography: High-quality photos that make your home shine online
  • MLS listing: Entered into Columbus REALTORS MLS, visible to all agents
  • Syndication: Automatically pushed to Zillow, Realtor.com, Redfin, Trulia, and 100+ sites
  • Yard sign: Professional sign with rider showing contact info
  • Lockbox: Secure electronic lockbox for agent showings

Your Role

  • Keep the home clean and show-ready
  • Secure valuables and medications during showings
  • Leave during showings (buyers are more comfortable)
  • Respond to showing requests promptly
Showing Tip

The first two weeks on market are critical. Maximum showing availability during this window often determines whether you get multiple offers.

What's in an Offer

  • Price: The offered amount (may be above, at, or below asking)
  • Earnest money: Deposit showing buyer's commitment (typically 1-2%)
  • Financing: Loan type, down payment, pre-approval status
  • Contingencies: Inspection, appraisal, financing, sale of buyer's home
  • Closing date: When title transfers (typically 30-45 days)
  • Inclusions: Appliances, fixtures, or items buyer wants

Negotiation Points

Everything is negotiable. Common counter-offer items include:

  • Purchase price adjustments
  • Closing cost contributions
  • Closing date changes
  • Contingency modifications or removals
  • Repair credits or as-is terms
Multiple Offers

If you receive multiple offers, we can ask all buyers for their "highest and best" to maximize your sale price and terms.

Timeline After Acceptance

  • Days 1-10: Buyer's inspection period. They may request repairs or credits.
  • Days 7-14: Appraisal ordered by buyer's lender to verify value
  • Days 14-30: Buyer's loan underwriting and final approval
  • Days 30-45: Final walkthrough and closing

Common Hurdles (and Solutions)

  • Inspection requests: Negotiate repairs, credits, or as-is with price adjustment
  • Low appraisal: Buyer pays difference, seller reduces price, or meet in middle
  • Financing issues: Extension, backup offers, or buyer brings more cash

At Closing

You'll sign the deed, settlement statement, and other documents. Proceeds are wired to your account (or applied to your purchase if buying simultaneously).

Before Closing

Cancel utilities for the day after closing. Remove all belongings. Leave keys, garage remotes, and any warranty documents for the new owner.
