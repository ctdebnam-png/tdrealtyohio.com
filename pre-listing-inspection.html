Know Before You List

Every TD Realty Ohio listing includes a professional home inspection before your property hits the market. No surprises, accurate pricing, smoother transactions.

What is a Pre-Listing Inspection?

A pre-listing inspection is a professional home inspection conducted before your property is listed for sale. A licensed inspector examines the major systems and components of your home, providing a detailed report of its current condition.

This is the same type of inspection that buyers typically order after their offer is accepted. By doing it first, you know exactly what buyers will find and can make informed decisions about how to proceed.

Included free with every TD Realty Ohio listing. This is not an add-on or upgrade. Every seller we work with receives this benefit at no additional cost.

Why Pre-Listing Inspections Matter

Knowledge is power in real estate negotiations. Here's how a pre-listing inspection benefits sellers.

Accurate Pricing

Price your home based on its actual condition, not assumptions. Homes priced accurately sell faster and for better prices than overpriced listings that sit on the market.

No Surprises

Eliminate the stress of wondering what a buyer's inspection will reveal. You already know, and you've already decided how to handle it.

Faster Closings

Deals are less likely to fall apart or be delayed when condition issues are already known and accounted for. Fewer re-negotiations mean faster closings.

Stronger Position

Transparency builds trust with buyers. When you disclose known issues upfront, buyers are less likely to request excessive concessions later.

What Happens After the Inspection?

Once you have the inspection report, you have options. There's no requirement to fix everything (or anything). You decide based on your goals and circumstances.

Option 1: Make Repairs

Fix significant issues before listing. This can justify a higher asking price and make your home more attractive to buyers who don't want a project.

Option 2: Adjust Pricing

Price the home to reflect its condition. Buyers who want a discount or don't mind making repairs themselves may see this as an opportunity.

Option 3: Disclose As-Is

Simply disclose the inspection findings and sell the home in its current condition. Many buyers appreciate the transparency and factor known issues into their offers.

We'll discuss the pros and cons of each approach for your specific situation. The right choice depends on your timeline, budget, and local market conditions.

A comprehensive examination of your home's major systems and components.

Appliances

Safety

Smoke detectors, carbon monoxide, GFCI outlets
