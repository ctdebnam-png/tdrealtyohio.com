About Upper Arlington, Ohio

City Overview

Upper Arlington is a city of approximately 36,000 residents located immediately northwest of Columbus and adjacent to The Ohio State University. Founded in 1918 as a planned community, Upper Arlington is known for its tree-lined streets, established neighborhoods, and strong sense of community. The city is completely built out, giving it a mature, settled character that distinguishes it from newer suburbs.

Upper Arlington's location provides exceptional access to OSU, Grandview Heights, downtown Columbus, and major employers throughout the region. The city has invested significantly in public spaces, including the recently renovated Lane Avenue commercial district and extensive park system.

Neighborhoods and Schools

Upper Arlington features distinct neighborhoods including South of Lane, North of Lane, and areas near Tremont. Homes range from Cape Cods and bungalows in the older sections to larger colonials and contemporary homes. Many original homes have been extensively renovated or replaced with new construction.

Upper Arlington City Schools is consistently ranked among the top districts in Ohio. The district operates one high school, two middle schools, and five elementary schools. The schools' reputation is a primary driver of home values in the community.

Landmarks and Attractions

The Lane Avenue commercial district offers dining, shopping, and entertainment within walking distance of many neighborhoods. Sunny 95 Park, Thompson Park, and numerous other parks provide green space and recreation facilities. The city's Labor Day Arts Festival is one of the longest-running community arts festivals in Ohio. Upper Arlington's proximity to OSU provides access to cultural events, athletics, and the university's extensive recreational facilities. The Scioto River provides scenic walking trails along the eastern border of the community.

Upper Arlington Real Estate Market

Market Conditions

Upper Arlington's real estate market is characterized by strong demand, limited inventory, and premium pricing. As a fully built-out community with top-rated schools, available homes generate significant interest. Many transactions involve either renovated original homes or new construction on lots where older homes have been removed. Competition for well-maintained, move-in ready homes is particularly strong.

Home Types and Pricing

Upper Arlington offers diverse housing from original 1920s-1950s homes to contemporary new construction. Pricing ranges from homes needing renovation in the $400,000s to new construction and extensively renovated homes exceeding $1.5 million. The pre-listing inspection we include is particularly valuable for Upper Arlington's older homes where systems may be original or aging.
