# TD Realty Ohio Voice Guidelines

## Tone
- Straightforward and professional
- Friendly but not overly casual
- Informative without being preachy
- Confident but humble

## Must Avoid
- No guarantees or promises about outcomes
- No invented or unverified statistics
- No steering language that implies certain areas are better for certain groups
- No protected-class language (race, religion, national origin, sex, familial status, disability)
- No superlative claims like "best," "top," "number one," "leading"
- No compliance-risk wording that could be interpreted as discriminatory
- No fair housing violations or language that could exclude any group
- No pressure tactics or urgency language ("Act now!", "Don't miss out!")
- No disparaging comments about other agents, brokerages, or areas

## Must Include
- Focus on factual property features and community amenities
- Highlight the service and value TD Realty Ohio provides
- Include the buyer credit program in an informative way
- Reference specific neighborhoods or areas only when provided in the brief
- Keep market observations general unless specific verified data is provided

## Language Style
- Use active voice
- Keep sentences concise
- Avoid jargon that buyers/sellers may not understand
- Be inclusive in all references to potential clients
