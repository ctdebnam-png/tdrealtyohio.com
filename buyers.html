When you're a first-time homebuyer working with TD Realty Ohio, you receive 1% of the purchase price back at closing. This comes from the buyer's agent commission, so it costs you nothing extra.

The cash back can be used toward closing costs, prepaid items, or any other purpose allowed by your lender.

The Home Buying Process

Click each step to learn what happens and how to prepare.

Why Pre-Approval Matters

  • Know your budget: Understand exactly what you can afford before falling in love with a home
  • Strengthen your offer: Sellers take pre-approved buyers more seriously
  • Speed up closing: Much of the loan paperwork is already done

What You'll Need

  • Last 2 years of tax returns and W-2s
  • Recent pay stubs (last 30 days)
  • Bank statements (last 2-3 months)
  • Government-issued ID
  • Social Security number for credit check

Types of Loans

  • Conventional: Typically 3-20% down, good credit required
  • FHA: 3.5% down minimum, more flexible credit requirements
  • VA: 0% down for eligible veterans and service members
  • USDA: 0% down for eligible rural areas
Pro Tip

Get pre-approved (not just pre-qualified). Pre-approval means the lender has verified your income, assets, and credit—it carries much more weight with sellers.

How the Search Works

  • Set up your search: Criteria based on location, price, size, features
  • Get alerts: New listings matching your criteria sent automatically
  • Schedule showings: Usually can see homes within 24-48 hours of request
  • Compare notes: Track what you like and don't like about each home

What to Look For

  • Overall condition and maintenance
  • Layout and flow—does it work for your lifestyle?
  • Natural light and orientation
  • Storage space
  • Signs of water damage or deferred maintenance
  • Neighborhood, noise levels, traffic

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Fixating on cosmetic issues (paint, carpet) that are easy to change
  • Ignoring location factors that can't be changed
  • Waiting for the "perfect" home in a competitive market
Pro Tip

Visit your top choices at different times of day. Morning light, evening traffic, and weekend noise can reveal things you'd miss on a single showing.

Components of Your Offer

  • Offer price: Based on comparable sales, condition, and competition
  • Earnest money: Deposit showing commitment (typically 1-2% of price)
  • Contingencies: Conditions that must be met (inspection, financing, appraisal)
  • Closing date: When you want to take ownership (typically 30-45 days)
  • Inclusions: Appliances, fixtures, or items you want included

Offer Strategy Considerations

  • How long has the home been on market?
  • How many other offers are expected?
  • What's the seller's motivation and timeline?
  • What contingencies are you comfortable waiving?

After You Submit

The seller can accept, reject, or counter your offer. Negotiations may go back and forth on price, closing date, contingencies, or other terms until both parties agree.

Pro Tip

In competitive situations, a strong pre-approval letter and flexible closing date can make your offer stand out even without being the highest price.

Timeline After Acceptance

  • Days 1-3: Earnest money deposited with title company
  • Days 3-10: Home inspection scheduled and completed
  • Days 7-14: Appraisal ordered and completed by lender
  • Days 14-30: Loan underwriting and final approval
  • Days 30-45: Final walkthrough and closing

The Home Inspection

A licensed inspector examines the home's major systems and structure. You'll attend the inspection (plan for 2-3 hours) to learn about the home and ask questions.

After the inspection, you can:

  • Accept the home as-is
  • Request repairs from the seller
  • Request a credit at closing
  • Cancel the contract (if inspection contingency allows)

The Appraisal

The lender orders an appraisal to verify the home is worth the purchase price. If it appraises low, you'll need to negotiate with the seller, bring extra cash, or cancel.

Important

Don't make major purchases, change jobs, or open new credit accounts during this period—it can derail your loan approval.

Before Closing

  • Final walkthrough: Verify the home is in agreed-upon condition
  • Review closing disclosure: Received 3 days before closing, shows all costs
  • Wire funds: Down payment and closing costs wired to title company (never via email instructions—always verify by phone)
  • Get insurance: Homeowner's insurance policy must be in place

What to Bring

  • Government-issued photo ID
  • Cashier's check or wire confirmation (if not already wired)
  • Any remaining documents requested by lender or title

At the Closing Table

You'll sign the mortgage documents, deed, and other legal paperwork. Plan for about an hour. Once everything is signed and funded, you'll receive the keys.

After Closing

  • Change locks immediately
  • Set up utilities in your name
  • File change of address with USPS
  • Keep all closing documents in a safe place
Congratulations

You're a homeowner! Keep your closing documents—you'll need them for taxes and any future refinancing or sale.
