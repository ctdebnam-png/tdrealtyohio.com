Discount Broker vs Full Service Agent

Understanding your options for selling your Central Ohio home. What do you actually need?

The Three Options

When selling your home, you'll generally encounter three types of real estate services:

Limited-Service Discount Broker

Lowest cost, reduced services

  • MLS listing
  • Basic or DIY photography
  • Limited showing coordination
  • Minimal negotiation support
  • No pre-listing inspection

Commission: $500-$2,000 flat fee

Full-Service Low Commission

Full service, efficient pricing

  • MLS listing
  • Professional photography
  • Full showing coordination
  • Expert negotiation
  • Free pre-listing inspection

Commission: 1-2%

Traditional Full-Service Agent

Full service, traditional pricing

  • MLS listing
  • Professional photography
  • Full showing coordination
  • Expert negotiation
  • Inspection usually extra

Commission: 2.5-3%

What Actually Sells Your Home?

Before choosing an agent type, understand what actually drives home sales:

1. MLS Exposure

The MLS feeds Zillow, Realtor.com, Redfin, and every other major site. All licensed agents can list on the MLS. This is the foundation—without it, buyers can't find you.

2. Professional Photography

Buyers scroll through listings online. Poor photos mean fewer showings. Professional photos are the difference between a scroll-past and a click.

3. Accurate Pricing

Overpriced homes sit. Underpriced homes leave money on the table. Market analysis and pricing strategy determine how quickly you sell and for how much.

4. Negotiation Skill

When offers come in, negotiation determines your final price and terms. Experience here directly impacts your net proceeds.

5. Transaction Management

From contract to closing, things can go wrong. Inspection issues, appraisal problems, financing delays—an experienced agent navigates these to keep deals together.

When Limited Service Makes Sense

A flat-fee or limited-service broker might work if:

  • You have real estate experience and can handle showings, negotiations, and paperwork yourself
  • Your home is in a hot market where it will sell quickly regardless of agent involvement
  • You're comfortable with the risk of leaving money on the table due to pricing or negotiation mistakes
  • You have the time to manage the process yourself

When Full Service Makes Sense

Full-service representation is usually better if:

  • This is your first time selling (or it's been many years)
  • You don't have time to manage showings and buyer communications
  • Your home needs strategic pricing due to condition, location, or market conditions
  • You want professional negotiation to maximize your sale price
  • You're selling and buying at the same time and need coordination

Questions to Ask Any Agent

Whether you're considering a discount broker or traditional agent, ask these questions:

Get specifics: photography, showing coordination, negotiation support, closing management. Don't assume—ask for the full list.

Will they coordinate all showings, or will you receive direct calls from buyer's agents? Who provides lockbox access?

How will they determine listing price? What comparable sales will they analyze? How do they adjust if the home doesn't sell?

Will they negotiate directly with buyers/agents, or just relay offers? What's their experience with inspection negotiations?

Who manages the timeline, coordinates with title, and handles issues that arise? Is this included or extra?
