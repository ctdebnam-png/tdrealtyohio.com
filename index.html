Common Questions Westerville Sellers Ask Before Choosing a Reduced Fee Listing

Reduced fee offers are frequently compared to flat fee MLS entries, referral sites, and traditional listing agents. The goal here is to address the decision points that typically block a consultation, without relying on unsupported claims. If you want these answers tightened to your exact process language, keep the question structure and adjust the wording.

Does a reduced listing commission change service quality?

A reduced listing commission does not require reduced representation if the service scope is defined and executed. The right comparison is whether the marketing, pricing strategy, negotiation, and closing coordination are covered, and what is excluded. Use the consultation to request the written scope for your listing so you can compare it to other options line by line.

Is a flat fee MLS listing the same thing as full representation?

A flat fee MLS listing often focuses on MLS entry and may leave pricing, negotiation, and contract execution to the seller. Some sellers prefer that approach, but others want representation through inspection responses, appraisal items, and closing coordination. This page is for sellers who want representation while still using a reduced listing fee structure.

How does the pre listing inspection affect pricing and negotiations?

The inspection gives a clearer view of condition items before the listing goes live, which can change how the home is positioned. It can also reduce the likelihood of late stage renegotiations after a buyer inspection, depending on what is found and how it is handled. The best use is to turn unknowns into a plan before offers arrive.

Can I get the 1% fee if I am not buying right away?

The 1% listing fee is tied to listing your home and purchasing your next home with TD Realty Ohio. If you are selling only, the page and calculator use the 2% sell only listing fee. If your timing is uncertain, the consultation is where you define what triggers the buy and sell plan.
