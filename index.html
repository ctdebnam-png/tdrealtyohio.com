About Westerville, Ohio

Westerville is located in Franklin and Delaware counties and is approximately 14 miles northeast of downtown Columbus. The city has an estimated population of around 41,000 and covers approximately 12 square miles. Westerville is served by Westerville City Schools, which includes several elementary schools, middle schools, and high schools. The city is known for Otterbein University and Uptown Westerville, a historic district with local shops and restaurants.

City of Westerville website Westerville City Schools

Westerville Market Overview

The Westerville real estate market in 2026 is characterized by stable inventory and consistent buyer demand. Most homes are single family residential properties, including contemporary subdivisions and older neighborhoods near Uptown. Listing prices vary by location, school district, and condition. Buyer expectations in Westerville include well maintained homes, updated systems, and disclosure of known defects.

When selling a Westerville home, pricing should be based on comparable sales from the past 3 to 6 months and reflect property condition. The pre listing inspection helps identify items that affect pricing and buyer response, and the goal is to reduce negotiation surprises by addressing condition items before showings.

Westerville Neighborhoods and Developments

Westerville includes a range of housing types and price points. Some popular neighborhoods and subdivisions include Cooper Farms, Summer Glen, Estates at Rocky Fork, Glen Willow, and Huber Village. Many of these subdivisions include amenities like pools, walking trails, and neighborhood parks, and proximity to schools and major roads is a selling point for buyers.

Older neighborhoods near Uptown Westerville include homes built from the 1940s to the 1980s and may include bungalows, ranches, and colonials. These properties are often on larger lots and may require updates to kitchens, bathrooms, and mechanicals. Location within walking distance to Uptown is a selling point and can support higher pricing compared to similar homes in outlying subdivisions.
