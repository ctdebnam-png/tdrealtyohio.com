About Powell, Ohio

City Overview

Powell is an upscale suburb located in Delaware County, approximately 15 miles north of downtown Columbus with a population of around 15,000 residents. The city is known for its excellent schools, family-friendly atmosphere, and proximity to both natural areas and urban amenities. Powell has experienced significant growth over the past two decades, transforming from a quiet village to one of Central Ohio's most sought-after communities. The Olentangy River runs along Powell's western edge, providing scenic beauty and recreational opportunities.

Major routes including I-71, US-23, and Liberty Road provide convenient access to downtown Columbus, the airport, and surrounding communities. Powell's location offers the perfect balance of suburban tranquility with easy access to Columbus employment centers, shopping, and entertainment.

Neighborhoods and Schools

Powell features a variety of neighborhoods ranging from established subdivisions with mature trees to newer developments with modern amenities. Popular areas include Scioto Reserve, Wedgewood, Highgrove, Brandywine, and the homes surrounding Powell's charming downtown district. Many neighborhoods feature community pools, walking trails, and proximity to excellent parks.

Olentangy Local Schools serves Powell and is consistently ranked among Ohio's top school districts. The district has received numerous accolades for academic excellence, and the schools are a primary driver of Powell's strong property values. Olentangy Liberty, Olentangy Orange, and Olentangy Berlin high schools serve different areas of the district.

Landmarks and Attractions

Powell's charming downtown district features local shops, restaurants, and community gathering spaces centered around the historic village area. The Columbus Zoo and Aquarium, consistently ranked among the nation's best zoos, is located just minutes from Powell and includes Zoombezi Bay water park and Safari Golf Club. Adventure Park at Zoombezi Bay offers additional family entertainment options. Highbanks Metro Park provides hiking trails, nature programs, and scenic overlooks along the Olentangy River. The Olentangy Trail connects Powell to downtown Columbus via a paved multi-use path perfect for biking and running. Antrim Park, Scioto Park, and numerous community parks provide additional recreation options throughout Powell.

Powell Real Estate Market

Market Conditions

Powell's real estate market remains strong due to the exceptional Olentangy school district and family-friendly community atmosphere. Buyer demand consistently outpaces inventory, particularly for homes in established neighborhoods with desirable school assignments. The market tends to be competitive, with well-priced homes often receiving multiple offers. Powell's combination of excellent schools, safety, and proximity to Columbus employers makes it a perennial favorite for relocating families.

Home Types and Pricing

Powell offers primarily single-family homes ranging from ranch-style homes to two-story colonials and custom-built properties. Home sizes typically range from 2,000 to 5,000+ square feet, with most featuring 3-5 bedrooms. Lot sizes vary from compact suburban lots to larger estate properties. Pricing reflects the premium buyers place on Olentangy schools, with Powell homes often commanding higher prices than comparable homes in other areas. The pre-listing inspection we include helps ensure your Powell home is priced accurately based on its actual condition.
