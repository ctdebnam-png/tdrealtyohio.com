About Delaware, Ohio

City Overview

Delaware is the county seat of Delaware County, located approximately 25 miles north of downtown Columbus with a population of around 40,000 residents. The city combines small-town charm with convenient access to Columbus employment and amenities. Delaware County has been one of Ohio's fastest-growing counties for years, and the city of Delaware has benefited from this growth while maintaining its historic character. Ohio Wesleyan University, a private liberal arts college, adds a collegiate atmosphere to the community.

US-23, US-36/37, and SR-315 provide access to Columbus, while proximity to I-71 makes commuting convenient. Delaware's location offers affordable housing with reasonable commute times to major Columbus employers.

Neighborhoods and Schools

Delaware features a charming historic downtown with Victorian-era homes, as well as newer developments on the city's edges. The downtown area near Sandusky Street offers walkable access to shops, restaurants, and community events. Newer subdivisions provide modern amenities while established neighborhoods offer mature trees and larger lots.

Delaware City Schools serves the city and surrounding areas with solid academics and community engagement. Some areas of Delaware County are served by Olentangy Local Schools, one of Ohio's top-rated districts. School district boundaries should be verified for specific properties.

Landmarks and Attractions

Delaware's historic downtown offers unique shopping, dining, and entertainment options centered around Sandusky Street. The Delaware County Fair, Ohio's oldest county fair, draws visitors each September with agricultural exhibits, rides, and entertainment. Alum Creek State Park and Delaware Lake provide boating, fishing, swimming, and hiking opportunities just minutes from town. Ohio Wesleyan University contributes cultural programming, athletics, and the Gray Chapel, a historic venue for concerts and events. The Olentangy and Delaware Greenway Trails connect the city to surrounding parks and communities. Preservation Parks of Delaware County operates multiple parks with nature programs, trails, and community events throughout the year.

Delaware Real Estate Market

Market Conditions

Delaware County has been one of Ohio's fastest-growing counties, and the city of Delaware continues to attract buyers seeking value and quality of life. The market offers more affordable options than communities closer to Columbus while still providing reasonable commute times. New construction and established neighborhoods both contribute to a diverse inventory. Growth in nearby areas like Powell and Lewis Center has increased interest in Delaware as a value alternative with its own distinct character.

Home Types and Pricing

Delaware offers everything from historic Victorian homes near downtown to new construction in developing areas. Single-family homes dominate the market, with ranch homes, two-story colonials, and split-levels all represented. Pricing tends to be more accessible than communities like Dublin or Powell, making Delaware attractive for first-time buyers and families seeking space. The pre-listing inspection we include helps ensure accurate pricing whether you're selling a century home with character or a newer suburban build.
