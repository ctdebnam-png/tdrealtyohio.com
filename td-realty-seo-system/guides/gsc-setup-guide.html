📚 Why Google Search Console Matters

Google Search Console (GSC) is a free tool that helps you:

  • Monitor indexing: See which pages Google has discovered and indexed
  • Track performance: See your rankings, clicks, and impressions
  • Find issues: Get alerted to technical SEO problems
  • Submit sitemaps: Help Google discover your content faster
  • Analyze keywords: See what search terms bring traffic to your site
💡
Time to Complete: About 15-30 minutes for initial setup. Verification may take a few hours to a few days.

1 Access Google Search Console

  1. Go to search.google.com/search-console
  2. Sign in with your Google account (use the same account as your Google Business Profile if possible)
  3. Click "Add property" in the top-left dropdown
⚠
Tip: Use a Google account you'll have access to long-term, not a personal account you might lose access to.

2 Add Your Website Property

You'll see two options:

Domain Property (Recommended)

Covers ALL subdomains and protocols (http, https, www, non-www)

tdrealtyohio.com

Requires DNS verification

URL Prefix Property

Covers only the exact URL you enter

https://tdrealtyohio.com

Multiple verification methods available

✓
Recommendation: Choose "Domain" property for comprehensive coverage. If that's too complex, use "URL prefix" with your full HTTPS URL.

3 Verify Ownership

For Domain Property (DNS Verification):

  1. Google will provide a TXT record to add to your DNS
  2. Log in to your domain registrar (GoDaddy, Namecheap, etc.)
  3. Find DNS settings and add a new TXT record
  4. Paste the verification code Google provided
  5. Save and wait (can take up to 48 hours to propagate)
  6. Return to GSC and click "Verify"

For URL Prefix (Easier Options):

HTML File Upload (Easiest) ▼
  1. Download the HTML verification file from Google
  2. Upload it to your website's root folder (public_html or www)
  3. Make sure you can access it at: yourdomain.com/google123abc.html
  4. Click "Verify" in GSC
HTML Meta Tag ▼
  1. Copy the meta tag Google provides
  2. Add it to your homepage's <head> section
  3. Example: <meta name="google-site-verification" content="abc123...">
  4. Click "Verify" in GSC
Google Analytics (If Already Installed) ▼

If you have Google Analytics installed with the same Google account, GSC can verify automatically. Just select this option and click Verify.

4 Submit Your Sitemap

  1. In GSC, go to "Sitemaps" in the left menu
  2. Enter your sitemap URL (usually sitemap.xml)
  3. Click "Submit"
💡
Common Sitemap Locations:
  • tdrealtyohio.com/sitemap.xml
  • tdrealtyohio.com/sitemap_index.xml
  • tdrealtyohio.com/sitemap/

If you don't have a sitemap, most website platforms (WordPress, Squarespace, Wix) generate them automatically. Check your platform's documentation.

5 Check Indexing Status

Quick Index Check:

Search Google for: site:tdrealtyohio.com

This shows all pages Google has indexed from your site.

In GSC:

  1. Go to "Pages" (formerly "Coverage") in the left menu
  2. Check "Indexed" count - these pages can appear in search
  3. Review any "Not indexed" pages - some are normal (redirects, etc.)
  4. Fix any errors (red) that are blocking important pages
✓
What to Look For:
  • Your homepage should be indexed
  • Key service pages should be indexed
  • Blog posts should be indexed
  • Neighborhood pages should be indexed

6 Monitor Search Performance

Go to "Performance" in the left menu. Key metrics to track:

Total Clicks

How many people clicked through from search results

Total Impressions

How many times your site appeared in search

Average CTR

Click-through rate (clicks รท impressions)

Average Position

Average ranking position in search results

Key Reports to Check Weekly:

  • Queries tab: See what keywords bring traffic
  • Pages tab: See which pages perform best
  • Countries tab: Verify traffic is from your target area
  • Devices tab: Mobile vs desktop breakdown

🔧 Common Issues & Solutions

"Page is not indexed" ▼

Common causes:

  • Page is new - give it time (days to weeks)
  • Page has "noindex" tag - check your CMS settings
  • Page blocked by robots.txt - check your robots.txt file
  • Page is duplicate content - canonical tag pointing elsewhere

Solution: Use "URL Inspection" tool in GSC to diagnose and request indexing

"Crawled but not indexed" ▼

Google found the page but chose not to index it. This often means:

  • Thin content - add more valuable content
  • Duplicate/similar to other pages
  • Low-quality signals

Solution: Improve the page's content quality and uniqueness

"Sitemap could not be read" ▼
  • Verify the sitemap URL is correct
  • Check if sitemap is valid XML (use a validator)
  • Ensure sitemap isn't blocked by robots.txt
  • Check for server errors (try accessing the URL directly)
No data showing in Performance ▼
  • New properties take 2-3 days to start collecting data
  • Very low traffic sites may show minimal data
  • Check you have the correct date range selected
  • Ensure site is actually getting search traffic
