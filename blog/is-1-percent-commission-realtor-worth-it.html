Is a 1% Commission Realtor Worth It? Columbus Home Seller Guide

By Travis Debnam, Licensed Ohio Real Estate Broker | Updated January 2026

You've seen the ads: "Sell your home for just 1% commission!" But is it too good to be true? Here's an honest look at what 1% commission realtors offer Columbus sellers.

What Is a 1% Commission Realtor?

A 1% commission realtor charges 1% of your home's sale price for listing services, compared to the traditional 3%. On a $400,000 Columbus home, that's $4,000 instead of $12,000—a savings of $8,000.

How Can They Charge Less?

Lower commission doesn't necessarily mean worse service. Many 1% brokerages save money through:

  • Lower overhead: No expensive office space or large staff
  • Technology: Efficient systems reduce administrative time
  • Volume: Some brokerages handle more transactions at lower margins
  • Direct service: Working without layers of assistants

At TD Realty Ohio, our 1% rate (when you buy and sell) comes from operating efficiently. You work directly with a licensed broker—no handoffs to assistants or junior agents.

What to Watch For

Not all low-commission options are equal. Ask about:

Hidden Fees

Some discount brokerages advertise low commission but add administrative fees, marketing fees, or transaction fees that reduce your savings. At TD Realty Ohio, our rate is all-inclusive.

Service Level

What exactly is included? Full-service should mean professional photography, MLS listing, marketing, negotiation, and transaction management. Anything less is a discount service, not just a lower commission.

Agent Experience

Is the agent a licensed broker or a newer salesperson? How many transactions have they completed? Experience matters in pricing, negotiation, and problem-solving.

Direct Access

Will you work directly with the agent, or be handed to assistants and call centers? Personal attention matters when selling your largest asset.

The TD Realty Ohio Difference

Here's what you get with our 1% commission:

  • Licensed broker handling your transaction personally
  • Pre-listing home inspection included
  • Professional photography
  • Full MLS listing and syndication
  • Strategic marketing
  • Expert negotiation
  • Complete transaction management
  • No hidden fees or surprise charges

When 1% Makes Sense

A 1% commission agent is worth it when:

  • You receive full-service representation
  • The agent is experienced and responsive
  • There are no hidden fees
  • You're comfortable with the agent's communication style

When to Be Cautious

Be careful if:

  • Services are vague or limited
  • There are multiple add-on fees
  • You can't speak directly with your agent
  • The agent seems overwhelmed with too many listings

The Bottom Line

A 1% commission realtor can absolutely be worth it—if you choose carefully. Focus on the total value: services provided, agent experience, and responsiveness. The commission rate tells you the cost, but the agent's quality determines whether you're getting a good deal.
