Selling Your Home in Central Ohio: Complete Timeline

By Travis Debnam, Licensed Ohio Real Estate Broker | Updated January 2026

Planning to sell your Central Ohio home? Understanding the timeline helps you prepare and set realistic expectations. Here's what to expect from start to finish.

Phase 1: Pre-Listing Preparation (2-4 Weeks)

Week 1-2: Interview Agents and Decide

  • Research and contact listing agents
  • Compare commission rates and services
  • Get comparative market analysis
  • Choose your agent and sign listing agreement

Week 2-4: Prepare Your Home

  • Pre-listing inspection (if includedâ€”TD Realty Ohio includes this)
  • Complete repairs and touch-ups
  • Declutter and deep clean
  • Professional photography
  • Create listing and marketing materials

Phase 2: Active Listing (2-6 Weeks)

Timeline varies by market conditions and pricing:

Week 1

  • Home goes live on MLS
  • Syndication to Zillow, Realtor.com, etc.
  • Initial showing activity

Weeks 2-4

  • Ongoing showings
  • Feedback collection
  • Price adjustment if needed
  • Receive and review offers

Columbus Market Note: Well-priced homes in desirable areas often receive offers within the first two weeks. Overpriced homes may sit for months.

Phase 3: Under Contract (30-45 Days)

Once you accept an offer, the closing process begins:

Days 1-10

  • Buyer's earnest money deposited
  • Buyer's inspection (if you had a pre-listing inspection, this often goes smoothly)
  • Inspection negotiation if needed

Days 10-25

  • Buyer's appraisal ordered and completed
  • Buyer's loan underwriting
  • Title search and insurance

Days 25-45

  • Final loan approval
  • Closing disclosure review
  • Final walkthrough
  • Closing day

Total Timeline: 10-16 Weeks

From deciding to sell to closing:

  • Best case: 8-10 weeks (quick sale, smooth closing)
  • Typical: 12-14 weeks
  • Extended: 16+ weeks (if home needs price reduction or has issues)

How to Speed Up Your Sale

  1. Price correctly from the start - Avoid the need for price reductions
  2. Get a pre-listing inspection - Prevent delays during buyer inspection
  3. Prepare before listing - Have repairs and cleaning done before going live
  4. Be flexible with showings - More access means more potential buyers
  5. Choose responsive professionals - Slow agents and lenders extend timelines
