Columbus Real Estate: Buyer's Agent Commission Changes 2024-2026

By Travis Debnam, Licensed Ohio Real Estate Broker | Updated January 2026

The real estate industry has undergone significant changes following the 2024 NAR settlement. Here's what Columbus buyers and sellers need to understand.

What Changed?

The 2024 settlement resulted in several key changes:

For Buyers

  • Buyers must sign written agreements with their agents before touring homes
  • These agreements specify what the buyer will pay their agent
  • Buyer agent compensation is no longer automatically offered through the MLS

For Sellers

  • Sellers are no longer required to offer compensation to buyer's agents through the MLS
  • Sellers can still choose to offer buyer agent compensation (and many do)
  • How compensation is communicated and negotiated has changed

What This Means in Columbus

In practice, the Columbus market has adapted:

Most Sellers Still Offer Buyer Agent Compensation

While no longer required, most Columbus sellers continue offering compensation to buyer's agents. Why? It attracts more potential buyers and their agents, typically resulting in better sale outcomes.

Compensation Is More Negotiable

The standard 3% buyer agent compensation is no longer assumed. Sellers can offer 2%, 2.5%, or negotiate based on their situation.

More Transparency

Everyone involved now has clearer understanding of who pays what. This transparency benefits consumers.

Strategies for Columbus Sellers

Option 1: Offer Standard Buyer Agent Compensation

Continue offering 2.5-3% to buyer's agents. This maintains maximum buyer exposure and is still the most common approach in Columbus.

Option 2: Offer Reduced Compensation

Offer less (2% or lower) and let buyers negotiate with their agents. Some buyers may cover any shortfall themselves.

Option 3: Offer No Compensation

Possible but potentially limits buyer pool. Buyers would need to pay their agent directly or negotiate for the seller to pay at closing.

TD Realty Ohio Approach: We discuss buyer agent compensation strategy with each seller based on their specific property, market conditions, and goals. There's no one-size-fits-all answer.

For Columbus Buyers

Key things to understand:

  • You'll sign a buyer agency agreement before touring homes
  • Your agreement will specify your agent's compensation
  • If the seller offers compensation, it typically covers your agent's fee
  • If the seller doesn't offer enough, you may need to make up the difference
