1% vs 3% Commission: What's the Real Difference?

By Travis Debnam | January 2026 | 10 min read

When selling your home, one of the biggest decisions you'll make is choosing a listing agent. For decades, the standard listing commission has been around 3%, but a growing number of agents now offer rates of 1-2%. The question many sellers ask is: what's the catch? Is a discount realtor worth it, or will you end up sacrificing service and sales price?

As a broker who offers 1-2% listing commissions in Central Ohio, I hear these questions regularly. Let me break down the real differences, debunk some common myths, and help you understand when each option might make sense.

  • Closing Coordination - Working with title companies, lenders, and other parties

What Some 3% Agents Might Add

Higher-commission agents sometimes justify their rates with extras like:

  • Professional staging (though many charge separately anyway)
  • Larger advertising budgets (but online exposure is largely equal through MLS)
  • Open houses (effectiveness is debatable; most buyers today find homes online)
  • Printed brochures and mailers (less impactful in the digital age)
  • Larger teams (which can mean less personal attention)

The question to ask: Are these extras worth $8,000-$15,000 more? For most sellers, the answer is no.

Debunking Common Myths About Discount Agents

Myth 1: "Your Home Will Sell for Less"

Reality: Sale price is determined by market conditions, property condition, location, and pricing strategy, not by the listing commission. A home priced right with quality photos and full MLS exposure will attract the same buyers regardless of what the listing agent charges.

Multiple studies have failed to find a correlation between commission rates and sale prices. What matters is having a skilled negotiator who understands the market, and that's about the individual agent's expertise, not their fee structure.

Myth 2: "Discount Agents Don't Work as Hard"

Reality: Low-commission agents often work smarter, not less. They use efficient systems, leverage technology, and focus on providing essential services that actually sell homes. Many have streamlined their businesses to cut overhead costs rather than cutting corners on service.

In fact, some high-commission agents with large teams may give you less personal attention because they're juggling many clients to support their overhead costs.

Myth 3: "Buyer's Agents Won't Show Your Home"

Reality: This myth stems from confusion about how commission works. The listing commission (what you pay your agent) is separate from what the buyer's agent receives. At TD Realty Ohio, we typically recommend offering competitive buyer's agent compensation to maximize showings.

Buyer's agents are legally obligated to show homes that meet their clients' criteria. The listing agent's commission has no impact on whether buyers see your home through Zillow, Realtor.com, or any other platform.

Myth 4: "You Get What You Pay For"

Reality: This common saying doesn't always apply to real estate commissions. The 3% standard was established decades ago when marketing a home required expensive print advertising and limited reach. Today, MLS exposure gives every listing equal visibility online.

What you should pay for is expertise, negotiation skills, and responsive service, and those qualities exist at every commission level. Check reviews, ask for references, and interview agents to evaluate their competence.

When a Traditional Agent Might Make Sense

While I obviously believe in the value of low-commission service, there are situations where a traditional agent might be worth considering:

  • Unusual or luxury properties that require specialized marketing beyond standard MLS exposure
  • Sellers who need significant hand-holding and aren't comfortable making decisions independently
  • Properties requiring extensive networking to find the right buyer (commercial, investment, unique use cases)
  • Existing relationships with a trusted agent who has specific knowledge of your situation

For the vast majority of residential sales in Central Ohio, though, a quality low-commission agent provides everything you need at a fraction of the cost.

Questions to Ask Any Agent, Regardless of Commission

Whether you're interviewing a 1% agent or a 3% agent, ask these questions:

  1. What specific services are included in your commission? Get a detailed list, not vague promises.
  2. How many transactions have you completed in my area? Local experience matters.
  3. Can I see reviews from recent clients? Past performance predicts future service.
  4. How will you price my home? Look for data-driven analysis, not just optimistic numbers.
  5. What's your communication style? Make sure it matches your expectations.
  6. Will I work directly with you or a team member? Know who's actually handling your sale.
  7. What happens if my home doesn't sell? Understand the listing agreement terms.
