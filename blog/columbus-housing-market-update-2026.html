Columbus Housing Market Update: January 2026

By Travis Debnam, Licensed Ohio Real Estate Broker | Updated January 2026

The Columbus real estate market continues to be one of the Midwest's strongest. Here's what sellers and buyers need to know heading into 2026.

Current Market Conditions

Columbus remains a seller's market, though conditions have moderated from the peak frenzy of 2021-2022. Key indicators:

  • Median home price: Approximately $325,000 (city) to $450,000+ (premium suburbs)
  • Days on market: 20-35 days for properly priced homes
  • Inventory: Still below historical norms but improved from recent lows
  • Interest rates: Elevated but stabilizing

What This Means for Sellers

Columbus sellers still have advantages:

  • Strong buyer demand continues, especially in desirable school districts
  • Well-priced homes still sell quickly
  • Multiple offers are possible in popular areas

However, sellers should be realistic. The days of every home getting 20 offers above asking price are over. Proper pricing and presentation matter more than ever.

Smart Strategy: In this market, a pre-listing inspection helps you price accurately and avoid surprises. TD Realty Ohio includes this with every listing.

What This Means for Buyers

Conditions have improved for buyers:

  • Slightly more inventory to choose from
  • Less competition on individual properties
  • More room for negotiation in some cases
  • Inspection and appraisal contingencies more commonly accepted

Hottest Columbus Areas

These communities continue to see strong demand:

  • Dublin: Top schools and corporate presence drive premium prices
  • Powell/Lewis Center: Olentangy Schools attract families
  • New Albany: Executive homes and excellent schools
  • Upper Arlington: Location and established reputation
  • Westerville: Great value with strong schools

2026 Outlook

Most indicators suggest Columbus will remain a healthy market:

  • Job growth continues with tech and healthcare expansion
  • Ohio State University anchors ongoing demand
  • Relatively affordable compared to coastal markets
  • Intel's Ohio investment may boost regional growth
