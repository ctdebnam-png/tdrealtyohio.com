About New Albany, Ohio

City Overview

New Albany is a planned community of approximately 12,000 residents located northeast of Columbus in Franklin and Licking counties. Developed beginning in the 1990s by the New Albany Company, the community was designed with a unified architectural vision emphasizing Georgian and Federal style architecture, extensive green spaces, and walkable neighborhoods. The result is one of Central Ohio's most distinctive and prestigious communities.

New Albany has attracted significant corporate investment, with Intel's planned semiconductor manufacturing facilities representing a transformative multi-billion dollar development. The community also hosts major facilities for companies like Abercrombie & Fitch and Bob Evans.

Neighborhoods and Schools

New Albany features carefully planned neighborhoods with consistent architectural standards, mature landscaping, and community amenities. Popular areas include the Village Center with walkable retail and dining, and established neighborhoods near the Country Club.

New Albany-Plain Local Schools is consistently ranked among the top districts in Ohio. The district is known for innovative programs, state-of-the-art facilities, and strong academic performance. The schools are a primary draw for families relocating to the community.

Landmarks and Attractions

Market Square serves as the community's downtown area with restaurants, shops, and the Philip Heit Center for Healthy New Albany. The New Albany Country Club offers golf, tennis, swimming, and social amenities. The community features over 50 miles of leisure trails connecting neighborhoods, parks, and amenities. Rose Run Park, Thompson Park, and numerous pocket parks provide outdoor recreation. The New Albany Amphitheater hosts concerts and community events.

New Albany Real Estate Market

Market Conditions

New Albany's real estate market is characterized by strong demand, limited inventory, and premium pricing. The combination of top-rated schools, planned community amenities, and corporate growth (particularly the Intel development) has made New Albany one of Central Ohio's most competitive markets. Well-priced homes often receive multiple offers.

Home Types and Pricing

New Albany homes primarily feature Georgian and Federal architectural styles per community design standards. Options range from townhomes starting in the $400,000s to estate homes exceeding $2 million. Most single-family homes fall in the $500,000-$900,000 range. Given New Albany's premium price points, the savings from our reduced commission are substantial.
