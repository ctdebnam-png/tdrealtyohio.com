About Columbus, Ohio

City Overview

Columbus is Ohio's capital and largest city, with a population exceeding 900,000 in the city proper and over 2 million in the metropolitan area. Located in central Ohio, the city serves as the county seat of Franklin County, though the metro area extends into Delaware, Fairfield, Licking, Madison, Pickaway, and Union counties. Columbus is home to The Ohio State University, one of the largest university campuses in the nation, which significantly influences the local economy, culture, and housing market.

Major highways including I-70 (east-west), I-71 (northeast-southwest), and the I-270 outerbelt provide access throughout the region. I-670 connects downtown to Port Columbus International Airport (John Glenn Columbus International), making the city accessible for business travelers and relocations.

Neighborhoods and Communities

Columbus offers diverse neighborhoods with distinct characters. The Short North Arts District features galleries, restaurants, and urban living. German Village, one of the largest privately-funded historic preservation districts in the nation, offers brick streets and restored 19th-century homes. Clintonville provides tree-lined streets and walkable access to parks and local shops. Grandview Heights and Upper Arlington offer established suburban communities close to downtown. Victorian Village, Italian Village, and the emerging Franklinton area provide additional options for buyers seeking urban living with character.

For families, Columbus City Schools serves much of the city, while suburban areas offer access to highly-rated districts including Dublin, Hilliard, Worthington, Upper Arlington, and Westerville.

Landmarks and Attractions

Columbus is home to major attractions including the Columbus Zoo and Aquarium (consistently ranked among the nation's best), COSI (Center of Science and Industry), the Columbus Museum of Art, and Nationwide Arena, home of the NHL's Columbus Blue Jackets. The Ohio State University campus anchors the University District with Ohio Stadium (the Horseshoe), the Wexner Center for the Arts, and extensive research facilities. Franklin Park Conservatory, Scioto Mile, and the Columbus Metroparks system offer green space throughout the region. The Arena District, North Market, and Easton Town Center provide dining, entertainment, and shopping destinations that enhance quality of life for residents.

Columbus Real Estate Market

Market Conditions

The Columbus housing market has experienced steady growth, driven by job creation in technology, healthcare, finance, and logistics. Major employers including Nationwide Insurance, Cardinal Health, L Brands, Huntington Bank, and Ohio State University provide economic stability. The city's relatively affordable cost of living compared to coastal metros continues to attract relocations from higher-cost regions. Inventory levels vary by neighborhood and price point, with some areas experiencing competitive conditions where well-priced homes attract multiple offers.

Home Types and Pricing

Columbus offers everything from historic homes in German Village and Victorian Village to new construction in emerging areas like Franklinton. Single-family homes dominate the suburban markets, while condos and townhomes are common in urban neighborhoods and near OSU campus. Pricing varies significantly by location: urban neighborhoods like Short North and German Village command premiums, while outlying areas offer more space at lower price points. The pre-listing inspection we include helps ensure accurate pricing whether you're selling a century home with character or a newer suburban build.
